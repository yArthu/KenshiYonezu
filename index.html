Kenshi Yonezu is a Japanese musician, singer-songwriter, and producer.
He was born on March 10, 1991, in Tokushima, Japan.

Yonezu began his music career in 2009, creating and uploading original songs
on a music sharing site called "Niconico Douga". He gained popularity through this platform
and eventually signed with a major music label in 2012.

Stray Sheep is the fifth album by Kenshi Yonezu, released on August 5, 2020.

Some of Yonezu's most popular songs include "Lemon", "Peace Sign", "Eine Kleine", and
"Uma to Shika". He has released several successful albums and has won numerous
awards, including the Grand Prix at the 61st Japan Record Awards for "Lemon" in 2019.

Lemon was released on March 14, 2018. It is the most famous song from Kenshi Yonezu.

Yonezu's music style is diverse, ranging from pop and rock to electronic and hip-hop.
He is known for his unique and powerful vocals, as well as his poetic introspective lyrics.
Yonezu is also skilled in producing and composing his own music, often
incorporating unconventional elements and experimental sounds.

In addition to his music career, Yonezu is also known for his visual artistry and has directed many of
his own music videos. He is considered one of the most influential and innovative artists
in the Japanese music industry.
